Tuvalu  
         
 

Tuvalu is known to be one of the smallest and most remote insular states in the world, surrounded by the vastness of the ocean, in the middle of the south sea. 8.000 natives live on a total area of only 26 square km, spread on 9 tiny atolls.

Tuvalu is also known under it’s former name „Ellice-Islands“. It was British protectorate from 1897 to 1978. With it’s independence it became the 38th member of the British commonwealth. Tuvalu is one of the least developed countries in the world. It’s economy is manly based on the trade of cobras. Stamps are the main source of income for foreign currency. Another important contribution is made by native seamen through international credit transfers.

  

medical facilities
A well equipped hospital with 31 beds can be found on Funafuti, the main island.

Air Marshall has a flight connection from Funafuti to Nadi (Fiji) three times a week. There is no local airline.

accommodation
There is only one hotel on TUVALU with 18 rooms and one conference room.

food
The hotel restaurant serves find fish dishes.

places of interest
There are no museums. You can admire thatched houses on most atolls.
