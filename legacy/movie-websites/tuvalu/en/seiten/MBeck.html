Michaela Beck SCREENPLAY WRITER  
       
 
The Berlin-born writer Michaela Beck began studying architecture in Weimar after finishing high school. Following her graduation there she worked from 1985 to 1991 as an architect, (Verlagsangestellte) (?) and then as an editor in a publishing house. From 1991 to 1995 she studied ‘Writing for Stage and Screen’ at the Academy of the Arts in Berlin (Hochschule der Künste). In 1992 she received a three-year scholarship from the Women’s Further Education Foundation or ‘Frauenstiftung’ (Böllstiftung). Further scholarships followed, such as the Berlin Senate Literature scholarship and in 1996 the Cultural Foundation Literature Scholarship. In 1998 she won third prize for “Sweet Naples” (Süßes Neapel”). She has been living since 1995 as a freelance writer in Ber-lin.

Her publications include stage plays, radio plays, radio features and screenplays.
