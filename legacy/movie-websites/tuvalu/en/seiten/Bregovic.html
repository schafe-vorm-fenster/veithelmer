Goran Bregovic ADDITIONAL MUSIC  
         
 
Born in 1950 in Sarajevo, son of a Serbian mother and a Croatian father.

After studies at the Conservatorium (violin) Goran founded his first band at the age of 16 and became part of Sarajevo’s colourful cultural scene. Here he met Emir Kusturica, at that time a bass guitarist in a punk band.

Goran studied philosophy and sociology. The success of his band “The White Button” made him one of Eastern Europe’s leading rock musicians. They toured for 15 years and released countless albums.

In the late 1980s Goran moved to a small house on the Adriatic coast and composed the music to “Time of the Gypsies”. The war shattered his peaceful existence there and he moved to Paris with his Bosnian wife. There he worked on Patrice Chereau’s film “La Reine Margot” (“Queen Margot”). He wrote the soundtrack to “Arizona Dream” und “Underground” for Emir Kusturica. More people bought the CDs than saw the film. For Radu Mihaelanu he wrote the music to “Train de Vie” (“Train of Life”). Veit Helmer showed Goran Bregovic the rough cut of TUVALU in an old cinema in Athens. Shortly thereafter Goran Bregovic called and made the track “Mocking Song” available for the film.

Since 1999 Goran Bregovic has been regularly touring Europe with his 45-piece “Wedding and Funeral Band” and appeared in Germany in summer 2000.
